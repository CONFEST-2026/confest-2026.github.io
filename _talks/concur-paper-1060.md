Guarded Kleene Algebra with Tests (GKAT) is a variant of
Kleene algebra which allows for reasoning about simple
imperative programs, and which features a decision
procedure for program equivalence in nearly linear time. In
the current paper, we address the challenge of reasoning
under assumptions about these programs. In particular, we
develop a form of Hoare hypotheses, which allow modelling
basic domain knowledge on pre- and postconditions of
uninterpreted basic programs, and which are well-developed
for classical Kleene algebra but not yet for GKAT. We show
that the resulting axiomatisation is sound and complete. We
then extend Hoare hypotheses to the more general form of
word hypotheses. Based on an automata-theoretic approach,
we show that equivalence of GKAT under word hypotheses is
as efficiently decidable as for plain GKAT.
